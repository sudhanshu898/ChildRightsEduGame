Responsibilities of Schools under RTE

  • Free Education: Schools must provide completely free education without any hidden charges. This includes textbooks, uniforms, writing materials, and other essential learning resources.
  • Infrastructure: Schools must provide safe classrooms, clean water, separate toilets, playground, and library.
  • 25% Reservation: Private schools must reserve 25% seats for economically disadvantaged students.
  • Zero Discrimination: Equal treatment regardless of caste, religion, gender, or economic background.
  • Safe Environment: No physical punishment or mental harassment.
  • Assessment: Regular, stress-free evaluations focusing on understanding over memorization.
  • Support Services: Additional assistance for children with learning disabilities and first-generation learners.
  • Parent Engagement: Regular meetings and participation in School Management Committees.

What If a Child Is Denied Admission?

If faced with admission denial, follow these steps:

  1. Document Everything: Keep all communication records, dates, and official names.
  2. Immediate Action: Request written explanation and contact RTE helpline.
  3. Escalation: File complaints with Education Officer and Child Rights Commission.
  4. Legal Steps: Consider legal notice, RTI application, or court approach if needed.

Note: Schools must respond within 15 days.

About the Right to Education (RTE) Act

Key Features

  • Constitutional Right: Makes education a fundamental right under Article 21A, ensuring free and compulsory education for all children aged 6-14 years.
  • Quality Standards: Sets specific norms for student-teacher ratios, school infrastructure, and teaching qualifications to ensure quality education.
  • Inclusive Education: Mandates integration of children from diverse backgrounds, including those with special needs and from disadvantaged groups.

Implementation Measures

  • Local Authority Role: Empowers local authorities to monitor school compliance and address grievances.
  • Teacher Training: Requires regular teacher training programs to maintain teaching quality and updated pedagogical methods.
  • Community Participation: Encourages active involvement of the community through School Management Committees.

Special Provisions

  • Age-Appropriate Admission: Children above 6 years who never attended school are admitted in age-appropriate classes with special training.
  • Transfer Flexibility: Students can transfer between schools without additional testing or certification requirements.
  • No Capitation Fees: Prohibits schools from collecting capitation fees or conducting screening procedures for admission.

Did You Know?

India became one of 135 countries to make education a fundamental right of every child when the RTE Act was passed in 2009.
