Responsibilities of Schools under the RTE Act

According to the Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009, schools must:

  • Provide free and compulsory education to children aged 6â€“14 years.
  • Ensure no child is discriminated against based on caste, religion, gender, or disability.
  • Maintain adequate infrastructure, including classrooms, toilets, drinking water, and playgrounds.
  • Employ qualified teachers and maintain prescribed pupil-teacher ratios.
  • Implement a child-friendly curriculum and assessment system, without holding back or expelling children until Class 8.
  • Reserve 25% of seats in private schools for children from economically weaker sections.
  • Establish School Management Committees (SMCs) involving parents and community members for school governance.

What to Do If a Child Is Denied Admission

If a child is denied admission, parents can take the following steps:

  1. **Approach the School**: Discuss the issue with the school authorities, citing the RTE Act.
  2. **File a Complaint**: Submit a formal complaint to the local education authority.
  3. **Seek Legal Assistance**: If unresolved, consult legal experts or NGOs specializing in education rights.
  4. **Escalate to Higher Authorities**: Approach the National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) or the State Commission for Protection of Child Rights (SCPCR) if necessary.
