School Responsibilities Under RTE

The Right to Education (RTE) Act, 2009 ensures free and compulsory education for children aged 6-14 years.

  • 📚 Provide free education, books, and uniforms
  • 🤝 No discrimination based on caste, religion, gender, or economic status
  • 🏫 Maintain proper facilities (classrooms, water, toilets, playground)
  • 💫 Reserve 25% seats for economically disadvantaged
  • ❤️ No corporal punishment or harassment
  • 📝 No failing or expelling students till Class 8
  • 👨‍🏫 Employ qualified teachers
  • 📊 Use continuous assessment instead of exams
  • 🌟 Support children with special needs
  • 👥 Include parents in School Management Committees

Teaching Children Aged 6-10

Special responsibilities for teaching younger students under RTE Act:

  • 🎨 Make Learning Fun – Use simple language, pictures, and activities
  • 🌈 Treat Every Child Equally – No discrimination of any kind
  • 💝 No Punishment – Create a safe learning environment
  • 📅 Track Attendance – Support regular participation
  • ✏️ Focus on Basics – Reading, writing, and math fundamentals
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Parent Updates – Regular communication about progress
  • 📋 Continuous Assessment – No pass/fail till grade 8

"Every child has a right to joyful learning! 📚✨"

General Teaching Guidelines

Do's ✅

  • 🎯 Create engaging learning environments
  • 🤝 Ensure equal treatment
  • 📊 Monitor progress regularly
  • 💡 Support struggling students
  • 📱 Maintain parent communication

Don'ts ❌

  • ❌ No physical punishment
  • ❌ No verbal abuse
  • ❌ No discrimination
  • ❌ No exam pressure
  • ❌ No neglecting special needs
